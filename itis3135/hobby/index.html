If you just want to go from A to B the best place to go would be the main roads, while it can be tempting to turn on the radio or put your own playlist of music on while driving, driving with the radio off can be theraputic or an oppritunity to collect your thoughts after a long day. If your looking to do somthing a bit more exciting, however, I have just the places for you. If your trying to learn how to drift (purposely oversteering so your car loses traction while maintaining control throughout the slide), I would suggest you go to an empty lot where you can LEGALLY drive and mark up the asphalt. Try asking the property owners of the lot or your local police officers if your allowed to practice, if they say yes then your good to go! I recommend that you try experimenting with slower speeds first until you get the hang of things.

How To Drive

Driving Itself is very easy and often necessary (In the US at least). The following instructions are for a automatic vehicle, if your looking to learn how to drive a manual you are on your own.

First, you get in the drivers seat. Then you put on your seatbelt and ajust your mirrors and seat to your liking. Next you put the key into the ignition and turn it clockwise, keeping the key held all the way untill the car turns on (dont hold it after the car is already on as this will damage the vehicle). Lastly, disengage the parking brake and put the car into gear! Which gear you engage depends on where your parked, if you need to back up shift the selector to reverse (R) and if you need to go forward shift into drive (D).
